Vlinder Academia

This is a demonstration of the Vlinder Academia theme for MkDocs. It was forked from Cinder, which has a more modern, sans-serif look. (Vlinder is Dutch for “Butterfly.” I could not think of anything else to name it.) The font families are EB Garamond and Victor Mono under an open font licence for that classic book look. Vlinder Academia is under MIT licence and you are welcome to personalize it or adapt it to another site generator.

The drop caps seen in the paragraph above are a bit of a hack; they’re implemented on the quote markdown operator (> text). The flourishes on headers and links are completely automatic. “Curled quotes” and apostrophes are only automatic when the smarty extension is enabled, or you may type them manually in the markdown.

There are some good MkDocs themes available, but none that quite nail that “academic paper from 1892” look I desire. I also find that many websites (including but not limited to the themes) simply set their text size far too small. Hence, I chose a good base and started hacking away at the CSS myself.

The colors were picked from the “crest” and “magma” Seaborn gradients. If you print this page (including to PDF), the accent colors will be flattened to black and white and the sidebars will be removed.

The author of this theme is 0xabad1dea (she/her, the hex is silent).

Installation and Use

Please see the Cinder docs for the nitty-gritty of installing the theme to your MkDocs (it’s not hard, just mentally substitute “vlinder” for “cinder”) and configuring some optional functionality that I frankly don’t care about.

Here are italics and here is bold and here is code and here is a link. The header at the top of the page is H1 and the one just above this is H2. Only the H1 and H2 headers are centered and colored, the others are left-aligned and black with no flourishes.

The above is a publisher’s flourish that was especially designed for texts typeset in Garamond, taken from this public domain specimen book. Images included through markdown syntax are centered and 75% of column width; use HTML markup if you need more control over how an image is displayed.

Note

This theme supports the “admonition” markdown extension for inline call-outs.

Warning

Without the extension enabled for your site, these will render poorly.

Danger

You can include arbitrary markdown within an admonition,

But the results may get a bit silly.

This shall serve both to demonstrate a basic code block and explain how to enable built-in extentions: simply add the following to your mkdocs.yml:

markdown_extensions:
  - admonition
  - smarty

The syntax for an admonition is as follows:

!!! note
    This theme supports the "admonition" markdown extension for inline call-outs.

Language Support

The fonts used in this template have complete support for extended Latin, Greek and Cyrillic, with generic system Garamond as the first fallback. Since I study Chinese, the fallback list also includes some Simplified Chinese fonts generally pre-installed on Windows, Android and Apple devices chosen to best match Garamond’s formal appearance. You can of course change them (in vlinder.css) to Traditional or Japanese fonts or add fallbacks for any other language. (I am not happy with the closest match I could find for iOS…)

More Dropcaps

Quote paragraphs have a minimum height to keep the capital from overlapping with things, and this may cause visually weird results if you throw around a lot of short ones. They also may not work as expected if they open with a bunch of punctuation. This is an arbitrary excerpt from Glory in the Thunder, which I, your theme maker 0xabad1dea, wrote:

As Barsamin fiddled with the fastener of his cloak, he stared openly at Hayr. “Pardon me,” he asked, “but are you… are you a Tokharika?”

Hayr took a moment to keep himself from being visibly insulted. “Well, my father was,” he said, “but my mother raised me to be a town man; I don’t even own a horse, or I’d be riding it now instead of walking all the way to Antaram.”

“… Oh.” Barsamin flushed deeper. “We are also going to Antaram… I, I suppose that was completely obvious though.” He folded his arms under his cloak. “It is indeed very cold here. How can you wear only that tunic?”
